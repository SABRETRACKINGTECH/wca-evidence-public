WCA Evidence

Privacy Policy

WCA Evidence is designed to minimize passive collection and keep the operator in control of evidence handling. This page explains what the app stores locally, what may be sent to a remote server, and what this build does not collect by default.

Last updated: March 2026

Terms of Use Support

What is stored on the device

Photos, videos, depth sidecars, OCR text, evidence metadata, and timestamped daily notes are stored locally in the app's Application Support container. Upload settings, such as the endpoint URL and case reference, are also stored on-device.

Protected storage

Saved evidence and notes use complete file protection and are excluded from cloud backup. If a bearer token is configured for uploads, it is stored in the iOS Keychain instead of plain preferences.

What gets transmitted

If the user initiates an upload, the app sends the selected evidence item, associated metadata, story text, and OCR text to the configured remote server. The operator of that destination is responsible for securing and governing that endpoint.

What this build does not do

This build does not include advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or in-app social sharing. It does not transmit evidence to the developer by default unless the configured upload endpoint is controlled by that same operator.

User choices

Users can keep material local-only, review stories before upload, clear the remote server configuration, and reopen the onboarding flow to review privacy and safety guidance.
